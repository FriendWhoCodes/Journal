## Pricing Psychology Deep Dive

### 1. Anchoring Effect

**Strategy:** Show individual prices first, then bundle.

```
Individual views: $9 + $9 + $9 + $12 + $15 = $54
Complete Pack: $29

"Save $25 (46% off) with Complete Pack!"
```

**Why it works:** 
- $54 becomes the anchor
- $29 feels like a steal
- Brain calculates savings, feels smart

---

### 2. Decoy Effect

**The Decoy:** Starter Pack at $19

```
Starter Pack: $19 (Week + Month only)
Complete Pack: $29 (ALL 5 views)
```

**Why it works:**
- User thinks: "For just $10 more, I get 3 extra views?"
- Starter Pack exists to make Complete Pack look better
- Some will buy Starter (still revenue), most upgrade to Complete

---

### 3. Price-Value Perception

| Price Point | Perception | Conversion |
|-------------|------------|------------|
| $9 | Impulse buy, low risk | High volume |
| $12 | Still affordable, slightly premium | Good volume |
| $15 | Premium, must be special | Lower volume, higher value |
| $19 | "Hmm, almost $20..." | Decoy effect |
| $29 | Sweet spot, "good deal" | Highest conversion |
| $49 | Serious investment, best value | Power users |

---

### 4. Charm Pricing

**Use $X.99 or round numbers strategically:**

- $9 (not $9.99) - Clean, simple, impulse-friendly
- $29 (not $29.99) - Professional, not cheap
- $49 (not $50) - Under psychological $50 barrier
